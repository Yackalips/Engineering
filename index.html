Week 10

        This week we learned about environmental engineering. environmental engineering focuses on how
        you can remodel something so it pollutes the Earth as little as possible. One thing that we 
        built that was related to environmental engineering was water filters. First, the dirty water
        goes through big rocks to catch big materials. Then it goes through sand, and 
        finaly cotton. Our filter was the second most efficiant and filtered the water pretty well.

Week 11

        This week was electrical engineering. We learned the difference between dc and ac circuits,
        how resistors work, and more. The first thing we did was build a parallel and series circuit
        in tinker cad.

Week 12

        We did more electrical engineering this week, but instead we built a real life model on a bread
        board. We built the ciruits and tested the voltages in different places.

Week 13

Week 10

This week we were doing an egg drop. We had to measure everything completley accurate and to scale. Our design 
    was based around an egg surrounded by cotton balls and sticks for support. For a soft landing, we put staws at the bottom.
    to make it fall slowly, we added a parachute (which ended up working really well), and a balloon on top to keep it upright.
    The egg we were givin had already started with a slight crack in it. When we first launched it, the wind blew the egg into a
    support beam so we had to start again. The second time, the egg flew down slowly and survived (minus the crack that kinda 
    got bigger)

Week 13

This week we actually started to cut the cardboard peices. It was not too hard, but there were a few changes we had to make. One of the changes was making the boat a bit smaller since before it was way too big. Another change we made was adding multople layers. This is so the boat has a less chance of sinking. Week 14

This week we made our prototype, but havn't actually tested it yet. The prototype is 4 times smaller than the actuall boat, so it should hold 1/4th our weight. Towa and I are going to be the ones that ride in the actual boat itself. We also cut out triangles and tapped a few things together.

Week 15

This week we made a prototype of the boat. The prototype was exactly in proportion, except it was 1/4 the size of the original boat. Surprisingly, it held all 5 bricks, but there was a smalle leak in one of the corners. So as long as we tape the actual boat well enough, we should be fine.

Week 16

This week we finished building most of the boat. We did not use that much tape at all and still have a lot left to use. We added a second layer on the bottom of the boat and also plan to add another layer to the sides. One thing we used for support was the segregator which also doubles as a table. It is a peice of cardboard which goes across the whole boat along with another peice under for support. We named it the segregator because it segregates both the people in the boat.

Week 17

For this week, we started to decorate the boat. Our boats name is the Burger King Shark, since brandons initials are BK, and Towa though the boat kind of looked like a shark. But this week we started to decorate it and make it look like a shark. We also added the burger king song lyrics to the boat.

Week 18

We finally finished building the boat! I would say it looks pretty good. There are still a few touch ups we need to make thought but I think it will work out good. We also made paper roller coasters this week. The goal was to make a roller coaster out of only paper and tape, and 2 loops. Only one of out loops worked, but the roller coaster still worked out pretty well.

Week 19

This is the second to last week of school! For our last challenge, we made a book holder. Our goal was to make a book holder out of only paper and tape, and make it so it doesn't tip over when punched. Our design was simpe. We made two triangles on the side, and the width being longer than the books hight preventing it from falling. To make the paper strong, I figured out how to make DIY cardboard by zig zagging paper and then surrounding it by more paper. Surprisingly it worked great and did not tip over.
